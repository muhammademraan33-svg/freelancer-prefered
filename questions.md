# Exam questions (extracted from images)

### Q1

Is it allowed to share contact details?

Ans- With the Recruiters permission, it is alright to share contact details with an employer. It is crucial to ask first.

### Q2

Is sending or stating your contact details or mentioning other sites where your information or portfolio are displayed, allowed?

Ans- You are allowed to share after a Recruiter project has been awarded. Should the project not be awarded you need to request permission from the Recruiter on the project first.

### Q3

True or false: You are allowed to provide contact details on your profile and/or bids.

Ans- False

### Q4

What should I do when an employer asks for contact details?

Ans- Write "Unfortunately, it is against T&C's and not advised to take communication offsite. In order to do so I need to first ask the Recruiter for permission."

### Q5

What should I do if the employer asks to pay directly using PayPal or another payment gateway?

Ans- Tell the employer payments should remain in the site and notify the Recruiter on the project.

### Q6

What should I do if an employer wants to have a Skype call about the project before it is awarded?

Ans- Contact a Recruiter and ask for permission to share offsite communication details with the employer.

### Q7

Would it be considered offsite communication if the employer shares their contact details first before the project is awarded?

Ans- Yes, should you need to engage with them offsite, you need to gain permission from a Recruiter first.

### Q8

Would it be considered off-siting if an employer asked you to use Team-viewer to discuss a project?

Ans- Yes

### Q9

When should you turn off your Preferred Freelancer Badge?

Ans- When you are unavailable for projects, going on vacation, or too busy to take on new projects.

### Q10

Where can you turn off your Preferred Freelancer Badge?

Ans- Under 'Settings' and then 'Account'

### Q11

Do you pay Preferred Freelancer project fees on Recruiter projects if your badge is turned off?

Ans- Yes

### Q12

Would you be penalised for not communicating with an employer that you would be away or need to delay the agreed time-delivery of a project?

Ans- Yes, you would penalised, as it is important to communicate with an employer and update them on the status of a project, especially should you not be able to complete a deliverable on time.

### Q13

What should you do when chat is opened for you by a Recruiter and the employer is unavailable?

Ans- Send a short message, introducing yourself and letting the employer know your working hours and when you will be available to discuss the project with them.

### Q14

What is an upfront payment?

Ans- Discussing a brief with an employer and once awarded the first milestone is requested before delivering any work.

### Q15

What happens when you request an upfront payment?

Ans- Withdrawals will be frozen until work is delivered.

### Q16

If you do research for establishing if you can take on a project, would you be allowed to request a milestone release for that without delivering any work yet?

Ans- No

### Q17

If you need to buy something for the project, be it a license, template or program to work on the project, what do you do?

Ans- Include it in the first milestone, but make sure that it is also followed by the agreed to deliverables. No milestones should be released without work done.

### Q18

You are allowed to charge 50% at the beginning of a Recruiter project?

Ans- No, that would be an upfront payment.

### Q19

Are you allowed to discuss project fees with employers?

Ans- No, discussion of fees is not allowed; You should always include the fee in the total price you give to the employer.

### Q20

What should you do if your employer wants you to create a secondary project from a Recruiter project, through ‘Hire Me’ or a normal project?

Ans- Notify a Recruiter about this as you were invited to this project via the Preferred Freelancer Program and let them know what the project ID is.

### Q21

What will happen when you circumvent fees?

Ans- You need to pay 30% of the awarded value of the project or be removed from the program.

### Q22

Are you allowed to request reviews or ratings before a project is completed?

Ans- You can ask for them once a project is completed.

### Q23

Would it be considered fee avoidance if a Recruiter hourly project was closed to create a non-Recruiter fixed-price project?

Ans- Yes

### Q24

How should you respond to an invitation to a project?

Ans- By telling the Recruiter about your experience and sending relevant examples of work.

### Q25

What would happen if you blackmailed, threatened or have unprofessional communication with an employer?

Ans- You will be immediately removed from the PFP.

### Q26

How should you FIRST address the employer?

Ans- Hi there

### Q27

Under what circumstances should you spam the employer with messages?

Ans- Never

### Q28

What percentage of disputes can you have in a six month period and still meet the minimum requirements to stay in the PFP?

Ans- 10%

### Q29

What percentage of projects do you need to accept to stay in the program?

Ans- 85%

### Q30

What percentage of completed projects do you need to maintain?

Ans- 85%

### Q31

What is a 'Timeout'?

Ans- A 'Timeout' is issued when you have broken the rules of the Service Agreement and will be given no invitations for three days.

### Q32

As a Preferred Freelancer are you allowed to consistently be awarded projects that you then break down into smaller parts for other freelancers to complete?

Ans- No

### Q33

Are you allowed to have a duplicate Preferred Freelancer account in the program?

Ans- No and if you do you will have to close one or both will be closed.

### Q34

If you are a company and not an individual what kind of photo should you have on your profile?

Ans- A professional company logo.

### Q35

What kind of photo should you have on your profile if you are an individual freelancer?

Ans- A professional individual photo.

### Q36

What if you want to add another skill to the Preferred Freelancer Program?

Ans- You will need to email preferred@freelancer.com.

### Q37

When is it OK to ask the employer to release a milestone?

Ans- Once you have delivered the work corresponding to it and the employer has already given you positive feedback.

### Q38

When should I accept the award and start working on the project?

Ans- After you have cleared every single detail of the project with the employer, updated your bid, if necessary, have agreed on a structure of milestones, and the employer has created at least one milestone.

### Q39

What happens if you have too many incoming milestones?

Ans- You will not receive Recruiter invitations until those projects are completed.

### Q40

What fees do you pay on an external Quote?

Ans- The Freelancer pays 3% fees and your Client pays 0% fees

### Q41

What is a scenario in which you should be sending quotes?

Ans- When you have an idea for a new piece of work that could help your client

### Q42

What is a scenario in which you should NOT be sending quotes?

Ans- To guarantee payments with users outside the platform

### Q43

Who should you send a quote to?

Ans- To a client that needs additional work done

### Q44

What is one of the differences between quotes and milestone requests?

Ans- Quotes can be sent to new external clients for a 3% lifetime project fee

### Q45

I sent a Quote to a client who I met on a Recruiter project that is still open and pending. What will happen to the Quote project?

- It will get upgraded to Recruiter and 15% is charged
